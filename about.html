The Story Behind Password Scrambler

Hi! I'm Mugisha Billy Chriss, a student in Rwanda studying Mathematics-Physics-Computer Science. Like many students, I use public computers at school, libraries, and internet cafes. I always worried about keyloggers stealing my passwords.

One day, while studying trigonometry (yes, math class!), I had an idea: what if you could type your password in a different order? The keylogger would capture garbage, but the website would still get the correct password through reordering.

I built the first prototype during school breaks, tested it with friends, and it worked! Now, Password Scrambler protects thousands of users from keyloggers, completely free and open source.

🎯 Mission

Make cybersecurity accessible to everyone, especially students and public computer users who can't afford expensive security solutions.
